Our first and core project is float, a user-controlled workspace and knowledge agent for working with AI using durable memory, tools, and personalization. It is designed to run locally or through compatible servers, so people can choose their own balance of privacy, capability, and convenience. More than a single assistant, each instance of float is intended to be a persistent, inspectable system for observing, learning, and conducting real computer work over a long time horizon. float learns by both updating simple files and databases that humans can collaborate on and embedding memories. float is extensible with modules for custom tools and skills to connect to your working environment and other agents. It is still early and actively evolving, but the direction is stable: a system that you can live, work, or make with.
